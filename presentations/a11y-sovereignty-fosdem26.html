Accessible Sovereignty: Why the Four Freedoms Depend on Inclusion

Sovereignty is a practice; accessibility is the power to exercise it.

Mike Gifford, CivicActions

9:35am, Feb 1, 2026 (UB5.230) | FOSDEM 2026
Community Track

I’m here to talk about a 'bug' in the way we view freedom.
Opening: Sovereignty is a discipline, not a product. So is inclusion. If you stop practicing, it atrophies. Inclusion operationalizes freedom. A legal right (the license) without functional power (accessibility) is an empty promise. Full submitted abstract is preserved in the comment above the cover slide.

The Core Premise

  • Foundation: Digital sovereignty is built on the Four Freedoms
  • Problem: Digital freedom is an illusion without accessibility
  • Thesis: Accessibility is the infrastructure that makes software freedom possible
  • Sovereignty = control; reliance on intermediaries erodes autonomy
  • If you cannot perceive/operate the tool, you are not sovereign
Define digital sovereignty as the ability for people & their communities to control & adapt the digital tools, data & infrastructure they rely on. You have no freedom without accessibility: “open without accessible” is exclusion — a library with the lights turned off vs a living, visible forest where everyone can use, study & share. Pair governance with tooling: just as DejaCode keeps licensing honest, ship accessibility automation (e.g., axe) in CI/CD so freedom is testable, not aspirational.

Re-evaluating the Four Freedoms

Requires the ability to:

  • Freedom 0 (Use): Perceive perceive the UI
  • Freedom 1 (Study/Edit/Improve): Operate operate & open it
  • Freedom 2 (Redistribute): Demonstrate understanding
  • Freedom 3 (Modify & Share): Refactor ideas & becoming more robust
Map 0–3 to POUR: P/O
Freedom 2: forges must be navigable & usable. Freedom 3: editors & IDEs (VSCode, Vim, etc.) & authoring platforms (ATAG 2.0). Cite FSF Four Freedoms: https://www.gnu.org/philosophy/free-sw.en.html#four-freedoms. If POUR breaks, the freedoms are theoretical.

Accessibility at the Foreground of Sovereignty

  • Accessibility long predates free software: a tradition of adaptive tech
  • Digital accessibility is the everyday practice of sovereignty
  • Both involve thinking beyond “me-now” & design for others
  • Accessibility & free software both expand choice over time
  • Always build for variation & resilience; preserve paths to maximum participation
Frame accessibility as the practical foreground of sovereignty—functional power to exercise rights. Barriers to access existed long before FOSS; see historical inventions: https://cs.stanford.edu/people/eroberts/courses/soco/projects/2005-06/accessibility/firstwave.html. Both accessibility & free software ask us to think bigger than the present self, giving the future “me” more options. Use POUR as the operational lens that keeps freedoms real across contexts.

Community is What Keeps Freedom Alive

  • FOSS thrives because people care enough to participate, modify & share
  • Accessibility thrives when communities care about others
  • If you cannot participate fully, you cannot help shape the future
  • It benefits us all to build for a wider variation in ability
  • Disability is innately human. We all have temporary & situational barriers
Show of hands: Is software freedom a human right?
Replace the Machine/Mycelium metaphor with community reality. Microsoft Inclusive Design lens: permanent, temporary, situational barriers all need coverage. Accessibility is the same adaptability that lets FOSS communities riff & personalize for today’s needs. Cite Microsoft Inclusive Design: https://www.microsoft.com/design/inclusive/. Everyone cycles through different ability states; building for variation protects future-you.

Inclusive Design: The Multiplier

  • Permanent: low vision, mobility limits, cognitive load
  • Temporary: broken arm, eye infection, jet lag
  • Situational: glare, bad Wi‑Fi, noisy room
  • Curb-Cut Effect: screen reader fixes often improve SEO & performance
  • Book Announcement: Digital Accessibility Ethics — Disability Inclusion in All Things Tech
These states rotate through a community. Building flexibility now protects the future-you & keeps the Four Freedoms usable when circumstances change. https://www.routledge.com/Digital-Accessibility-Ethics-Disability-Inclusion-in-All-Things-Tech/Feingold-Gilbert-Fleet/p/book/9781041018681

Governance for Accessibility

  • Ship accessible defaults & review checklists, pattern libraries by default
  • Upstream-first fixes prevent forks of inaccessible code
  • Require accessibility checks in build workflows
  • Assign an accessibility champion to keep the discipline funded, accountable & reinforced by leadership

Treat Accessibility Issues as Bugs

  • When people cannot report bugs or suggest fixes, Freedom 3 is lost
  • Exclusion from contribution turns sovereign users into passive consumers
  • Fix the gap: accessible editors, forges & involve people with lived experience of disability
  • GitHub: Fix Accessibility Bugs
Participation gates define who holds power. If you cannot use the tools or contribute, the Four Freedoms are hollow.

Sunlight is the Best Disinfectant

  • Open, accessible collaboration is the engine of sovereignty
  • Contribution workflows must be perceivable, operable, understandable & robust (POUR)
  • Accessible issue queues prove progress toward perfection
  • Participation gates define who holds power. Make contribution pipelines inclusive.

Accessibility & Sustainability


We are in a climate crisis so do want to raise how climate relates to accessibility.
  • Lightweight code is green & accessible
  • Emergencies demand tools that work for everyone
  • Transparency: do not hide barriers; report them publicly
  • Web Sustainability Guidelines currently touches on both open & accessibility
Cite Digital Accessibility Ethics; align eco-efficiency with inclusion.

Prioritize Building an Inclusive Community

  • Ensure your team is engaging in upstream bugs
  • Hire developers with disabilities
  • Ship with accessibility mode enabled by default
  • Require accessibility sign-off in PR templates
Closing: Sovereignty and inclusion are practiced.
Fund the roots, or the forest dies. End with action: money, policy, hiring. Inclusion is the practice that sustains freedom.
