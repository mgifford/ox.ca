Hook: June 28, 2025 has passed; if you shipped this morning without WCAG 2.2 checks, you may already be out of compliance.

The EAA Is the New Floor

  • EAA (Directive 2019/882) extends beyond public sector; it covers services and products.
  • Fines now reach up to €1,000,000 in some member states.
  • EN 301 549: If your Drupal site sells a ticket but your help desk is inaccessible, you fail.

Frame EAA as baseline, not ceiling. Move beyond WAD scope; emphasize service-wide compliance.

Web Almanac 2024 — The Hard Truth

  • Lighthouse scores trend up.
  • 30% of mobile sites still fail color contrast.
  • We are winning the automated war, losing the human one.

Reference Mike’s research; highlight gap between automation and real usability.

WCAG 2.2 — The New Nine

  • Focus appearance: visible, obvious, persistent.
  • Dragging movements: offer tap/click alternatives for tasks like missed-bin reports.
  • Target size: the thumb test (at least 24x24 px).

Stress pragmatic impacts for Drupal editors and themers; no hidden focus rings.

The One-Minute Audit

Close one eye, hold your phone at arm's length, and try to find the “Search” button with your thumb.

If you missed, you just failed WCAG 2.2 2.5.8. How many citizens miss daily?

Invite the room to actually do it; anchor the human impact.

LocalGov Drupal — The Fellowship of the Code

  • LGD targets WCAG 2.2 AA out of the box.
  • Public good: shared patterns and governance reduce per-council risk.
  • Example: Southwark PDF Importer turns inaccessible PDFs into accessible HTML for everyone.

EAA makes accessibility a market requirement; LGD is how the public sector meets it at scale.

Reinforce collaboration as compliance multiplier; less rework, shared upgrades.

Strategic Procurement (EN 301 549)

  • Stop buying debt; require VPAT 2.4 (EU) evidence.
  • Mandate use of the LGD Pattern Library; no bespoke bugs on public money.
  • Ask for upstream history, not just “we know accessibility.”

Procurement as control point; align contracts to accessibility outcomes.

AI & Accessibility — Friend or Foe?

  • Use AI for drafts: alt-text suggestions, structure checks.
  • Never outsource final judgment; humans feel friction, AI does not.
  • Keep data local; respect privacy and procurement constraints.

Balance automation with human testing; caution on overlays and hallucinations.

Connect legal driver to shared delivery model; emphasize multiplier effect.
